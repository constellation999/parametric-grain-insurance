# The Mechanism in 12 Steps

## Setup (before any crisis)
1. **Enroll through co-ops and MFIs.** Two member classes: food buyers (covered against price rises) and farmers (against falls). The poor buy insurance from people they trust, not from apps.
2. **Net the two books.** Buyers and sellers are hurt by opposite price moves, so their coverage partially cancels — only the net exposure needs external capital.
3. **Build the capital stack, cheapest first.** Grain traders sell price caps (paid from their crisis windfalls, secured by warehouse receipts) → CME call options cover the systematic rest → donor money sits as first-loss in an on-chain escrow that releases *only* on public triggers, so no official can delay or divert it.
4. **Sign a diversified supply network.** Forward contracts with producers across uncorrelated regions, plus pre-positioned stock. Suppliers deliver grain at payout time — but never report prices (no oracle conflicts of interest).
5. **Fix the oracles in advance.** Satellite indices (NDVI, rainfall, soil moisture) + entitlement ratios (wage ÷ grain price). Multiple indices must agree; no committees.

## Quiet years
6. **Monitor and recycle.** Premiums pay for hedges; a share goes to a quadratic-funding round that builds public goods — better indices, prevention — with donor matching following members' own contributions.

## Crisis
7. **Satellites fire first** → automatic *partial early payout*, weeks before prices peak — preventing the fire-sale of assets that turns a bad season into a poverty trap.
8. **Price ratios confirm** → main payout releases automatically. No claims, no adjusters, no votes.
9. **The waterfall pays in order:** reserves → hedger caps → options → donor first-loss. Each layer pays in the state where its money is cheapest.
10. **Aggregators distribute** cash + physical grain, using local knowledge of who was actually hurt — absorbing the index's basis risk at the last mile.

## Aftermath
11. **Track record compounds.** Visible, on-time payouts are the strongest known driver of future demand.
12. **Recalibrate.** Observed gaps feed the next funding round for index and supply improvements.

---

**One sentence:** Fix the promise before the crisis; let satellites and markets — never committees — decide when it is kept; pay through the people the poor already trust; and fund each layer of risk with the money that is cheapest exactly when it must pay.
